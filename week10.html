Week 10: Psychological Disorders

Introduction to Psychological Disorders

Psychological disorders, also known as mental disorders or mental illnesses, are conditions characterized by abnormal thoughts, feelings, and behaviors that cause significant distress or impairment in functioning. Understanding psychological disorders is crucial for recognizing mental health issues and providing effective treatment.

Overview of Major Psychological Disorders

1. Anxiety Disorders

  • Definition: Characterized by excessive fear or anxiety that interferes with daily activities.
  • Types:
    • Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD): Persistent and excessive worry about various aspects of life.
    • Panic Disorder: Recurrent panic attacks characterized by sudden onset of intense fear or discomfort.
    • Social Anxiety Disorder: Intense fear of social situations where one may be judged or embarrassed.
    • Specific Phobias: Irrational fears of specific objects or situations (e.g., heights, spiders).

2. Mood Disorders

  • Definition: Disorders that primarily affect emotional state.
  • Types:
    • Major Depressive Disorder: Persistent feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and loss of interest in activities.
    • Bipolar Disorder: Characterized by alternating episodes of depression and mania (elevated mood and energy).

3. Personality Disorders

  • Definition: Enduring patterns of behavior, cognition, and inner experience that deviate from cultural expectations.
  • Types:
    • Borderline Personality Disorder: Characterized by instability in relationships, self-image, and emotions.
    • Antisocial Personality Disorder: Disregard for the rights of others; often involves deceitful or manipulative behavior.

4. Psychotic Disorders

  • Definition: Severe mental disorders characterized by a disconnection from reality.
  • Key Example:
    • Schizophrenia: Involves hallucinations (false perceptions), delusions (false beliefs), and disorganized thinking.

5. Obsessive-Compulsive and Related Disorders

  • Definition: Characterized by the presence of obsessions (intrusive thoughts) and compulsions (repetitive behaviors).
  • Key Example:
    • Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD): Individuals feel driven to perform certain rituals to reduce anxiety related to their obsessions.

Diagnostic Criteria

The Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders (DSM-5) is the primary reference used by mental health professionals for diagnosing psychological disorders. Key components include:

  1. Criteria Sets: Specific symptoms that must be present for a diagnosis.
  1. Duration: Symptoms must persist for a specified period.
  1. Functional Impairment: Symptoms must cause significant distress or impairment in social, occupational, or other important areas of functioning.

Treatment Approaches

1. Psychotherapy

  • Definition: A therapeutic approach that involves talking with a trained mental health professional to address emotional and psychological issues.
  • Types of Therapy:
    • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT): Focuses on identifying and changing negative thought patterns and behaviors.
    • Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT): A form of CBT specifically designed for individuals with borderline personality disorder; emphasizes emotional regulation and interpersonal effectiveness.
    • Exposure Therapy: A behavioral therapy used to treat phobias and anxiety disorders through gradual exposure to feared stimuli.

2. Medication

  • Medications can help manage symptoms of psychological disorders. Common classes include:
    • Antidepressants: Used to treat depression and anxiety disorders (e.g., SSRIs like fluoxetine).
    • Antipsychotics: Used to manage symptoms of psychosis (e.g., risperidone).
    • Anxiolytics: Medications that reduce anxiety (e.g., benzodiazepines).

3. Integrated Approaches

  • Many treatment plans combine psychotherapy and medication for a more comprehensive approach to managing psychological disorders.

Applications in Social Psychology

Understanding psychological disorders is essential for addressing stigma and promoting mental health awareness in society. It helps in creating supportive environments for individuals with mental health issues.

Applications in Industrial Psychology

In organizational settings:

  • Awareness of mental health issues can inform employee assistance programs (EAPs) to support staff struggling with psychological disorders.
  • Understanding how stress affects mental health can lead to better workplace policies promoting well-being.
