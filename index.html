Psychology Syllabus

Course Title

Introduction to Psychology

Course Description

This course provides an overview of the fundamental concepts, theories, and research in psychology. Students will explore various psychological perspectives, including biological, cognitive, behavioral, and social approaches to understanding human behavior.

Course Objectives

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

  1. Understand key psychological concepts and terminology.
  1. Analyze different psychological theories and their applications.
  1. Evaluate research methods used in psychology.
  1. Apply psychological principles to everyday situations.
  1. Develop critical thinking skills related to psychological topics.

Weekly Topics

Week 1: Introduction to Psychology

  • Overview of psychology as a science
  • History and evolution of psychology
  • Major fields and applications of psychology

Week 2: Research Methods in Psychology

  • Types of research methods (e.g., experiments, surveys, observational studies)
  • Ethical considerations in psychological research
  • Data analysis and interpretation

Week 3: Biological Bases of Behavior

  • The structure and function of the nervous system
  • Brain anatomy and its role in behavior
  • Neurotransmitters and hormones

Week 4: Sensation and Perception

  • The process of sensation and perception
  • Theories of perception (e.g., Gestalt principles)
  • Factors influencing perception

Week 5: Learning Theories

  • Classical conditioning (Pavlov)
  • Operant conditioning (Skinner)
  • Observational learning (Bandura)

Week 6: Memory

  • Models of memory (e.g., multi-store model)
  • Types of memory (short-term, long-term, working memory)
  • Factors affecting memory retention and recall

Week 7: Developmental Psychology

  • Stages of human development (e.g., Piaget, Erikson)
  • Nature vs. nurture debate
  • Key developmental milestones

Week 8: Personality Theories

  • Major personality theories (e.g., Freud, Jung, Rogers)
  • Assessing personality (e.g., personality tests)
  • The role of culture in personality development

Week 9: Social Psychology

  • Group dynamics and behavior
  • Attitudes, persuasion, and conformity
  • Prejudice and discrimination

Week 10: Psychological Disorders

  • Overview of major psychological disorders (e.g., anxiety, depression)
  • Diagnostic criteria (DSM-V)
  • Treatment approaches (therapy, medication)

Week 11: Health Psychology

  • The relationship between psychology and physical health
  • Stress management techniques
  • Coping strategies for mental well-being

Week 12: Final Review and Exam Preparation

  • Review key concepts from the course
  • Discussion on exam format and expectations

Assessment Methods

  1. Quizzes - Weekly quizzes to assess understanding of material.
  1. Midterm Exam - A comprehensive exam covering the first half of the course.
  1. Final Exam - A comprehensive exam covering all course content.
  1. Research Paper - A paper on a chosen topic in psychology that demonstrates research skills.
  1. Participation - Active participation in class discussions and activities.
